Performed by:

Felix Mendelssohn's Violin Concerto in E minor, Op. 64, is his last large orchestral work. It is one of the most lyrical and flowing works of its type and one of the most frequently performed of all violin concertant part of the violin repertoire. Tchaikovsky - Violin Concerto in D major

Performed By:
DIOSPYROS
Joint Performance By:
Anna Hannah

The Violin Concerto in D was written in 1878 during the time almost immediately after Tchaikovsky fled from his failed marriage. The concerto was created after Tchaikovsky met with his old friend, Joseph Kotek, in Switzerland. Together they played Lalo’s Symphony Espagnole which gave Tchaikovsky the inspiration. Now performed by DIOSPYROS, we can recollect the work of a great music master.
